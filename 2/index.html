Coyy Sake

The Branding and packaging design for Coyy's premium non-alcoholic Eternal Blossom Sake features a harmonious blend of traditional Japanese aesthetics and modern artistry. Dominated by a serene, greenish-blue backdrop, the label showcases two vibrant koi fish swimming gracefully, symbolizing perseverance and good fortune. The intricate water patterns and detailed depiction of the koi add a dynamic, fluid element, enhancing the sense of elegance and tranquility. The minimalist yet bold typography emphasizes the brand's name, creating a striking contrast against the artwork. Overall, the design encapsulates Coyy's dedication to tradition, craftsmanship, and the refined beauty of Japanese culture.

Aozan Green Plum Wine

The thought process behind Aozan Green Plum Wine's packaging design focuses on emphasizing the natural and authentic essence of the product. The use of green plums and blossoming branches in the illustration conveys freshness and a connection to nature, highlighting the key ingredient. This choice reflects the traditional craftsmanship and natural ingredients used in the wine. The elegant, hand-drawn style of the artwork paired with soft, earthy tones creates a sophisticated and inviting appearance. The bold typography ensures brand visibility while maintaining a harmonious balance with the delicate illustrations, representing the refined and high-quality nature of Aozan Green Plum Wine

Book Redesign - The Red Book by Carl Jung

For the redesign of "The Red Book" by C.G. Jung, I utilized intricate mandala patterns to reflect Jung's exploration of the psyche and the book’s deep, introspective nature. The rich, dark color palette with gold accents conveys a sense of mystery and sophistication, while the detailed, symmetrical design symbolizes the inner workings of the mind. The bold, serif typography adds an authoritative and classic touch, ensuring readability. The composition centers on the elaborate mandala, drawing the viewer's eye to the title and creating a visually striking cover that embodies the profound and analytical essence of Jung’s work

Album Redesign - Roslyn by Bon Iver

The redesign of the "Roslyn" cover by Bon Iver and St. Vincent features a minimalist and moody aesthetic to reflect the haunting and atmospheric nature of the song. The dark background sets a somber tone, while the abstract brushstroke adds a sense of fluidity and emotion. The serif typography used for "Roslyn" evokes a sense of timelessness and elegance, complementing the melancholic theme of the track. The subtle inclusion of the "Twilight" branding ties it back to the "New Moon" soundtrack, maintaining its connection to the original context. Overall, the design aims to visually capture the song's introspective and ethereal qualities.

Twilight Cafe

The branding for Twilight Cafe seamlessly integrates the cozy ambiance of a cafe with the intellectual charm of a bookstore. Inspired by the twilight hours, the design uses a rich and muted color palette, featuring deep blues, warm browns, and soft beiges, to evoke a sense of warmth and comfort. The logo, depicting a steaming coffee cup with book spines, encapsulates the dual essence of the cafe and bookstore.

Actually Good, Girl Dinner - Cookbook

Actually Good, Girl Dinner reimagines the playful trend of “girl dinner” by transforming it from a random plate of snacks into a collection of globally inspired comfort meals that are both simple and satisfying. This cookbook features easy-to-make recipes from around the world, each designed for minimal effort and maximum flavor. It’s perfect for busy college students, solo diners, or anyone who craves a low-maintenance meal that still feels indulgent. By celebrating dishes rooted in comfort and culture, this book proves that you don’t need fancy techniques or hard-to-find ingredients to create an actually good dinner.
